About Padova

Padova is a city in North Eastern Italy. It is located centrally in the Veneto region, between Venice on one side and Vicenza and Verona on the other. Padova is called the city with the 3 without.Here are the 'withouts':

  • Il Prato senza erba (the lawn without grass). It is Prato della Valle, one of the biggest squares in Europe
  • Il Santo senza nome (The Saint with no name). It refers to the Basilica of St. Anthony in Padua. In town, we consider Saint Anthony the saint par excellence and therefore we have no need to name him.
  • Il Caffè senza porte (the café without doors). It is the Pedrocchi Cafè. Once it never closed, day and night (until 1916) so it had no doors. Now it closes at night and plus it has glass transparent doors.

  2. Eat ice-cream from Gelateria La Romana
  3. Visit the Scrovegni Chapel
  4. Ride a bike along the river
  5. Go on a romantic date at Specola
  6. Taste a real cappucino at the Cafe Diemme
  7. Try the traditional Polenta!
  8. Drink an aperol spritz at Giardini dell'arena
  9. Buy pasta Bigoli in a cup

Daily trips from Padova

Padova is a central railway node in the Veneto area. Many lines converge into the city central station, notably from:

  • Padova - Venice is only a 30-minute ride away.
  • Padova - Bologna is one hour and half ride.
  • Padova - Milano is only two hours and half minute ride.
  • Padova - Verona is 45 minute ride.
